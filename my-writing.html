After

An author of books about punctuation, Keith Houston, in his BBC article, The Mysterious Origins of Punctuation, published on 2nd September 2015 by the BBC, addresses the topic of the history of punctuation and argues that we need punctuation more now than ever before. He supports this claim by giving reasons why speaking used to be more important than reading, then giving the reasons why punctuation began to appear, then how punctuation evolved to become more and more useful, and finally noting that though punctuation had occasionally been removed in some places, it always came back. He also noted that as societies became more powerful and advanced, punctuation was more complicated as a result, implying that punctuation is needed to convey complex ideas more accurately. Noted also is the fact that for a long time, punctuation has remained stagnant and without change, though society continues to develop. He argues that this means punctuation is likely to undergo change sometime soon. Also conveyed is the idea that punctuation will change again soon because other forms of written text are developing, such as typing and emojis, and punctuation needs to catch up. Houston’s purpose in writing the article is to educate readers on how punctuation became commonplace all around the world. He adopts an academic and informative tone for his audience, namely the readers of the BBC, and others interested in the topic of the origins of punctuation. This could also include people interested in punctuation or the writing system as a whole.
